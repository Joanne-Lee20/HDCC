GNRA312: Generative AI for Thinkers and Makers

Course Syllabus

Tu/Thu: 11:00AM-12:15PM 3 credits

I. Course Description:

Generative Artificial Intelligence (AI) has rapidly become a captivating field that influences numerous aspects of our lives, from art and entertainment to healthcare and technology. Throughout this course, participants will explore the fascinating world of generative AI, learning about its underlying principles, applications, and ethical considerations. No prior knowledge of programming or AI is required, as the course is designed to be accessible and engaging for the general public.

II. Course Objectives:

Date Objectives Readings
Week 1 What is Generative AI? In week 1, we will define the term “Generative AI”, dive into its core concepts, and explore its extensive history. Reading 1
Week 2 How Does Generative AI Work? In week 2, we will explain the processes behind Generative AI. Reading 2
Week 3 Models of Generative AI. In week 3, analyze the different types of models of Generative AI. Reading 3
Week 4 Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) and Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) Reading 4
Week 5 Evaluating Generative AI Models. Breaks down what makes a successful Generative AI model. Reading 5
Week 6 Applications of Generative AI. Explains the importance of Generative AI, its case uses, and possibilities. Reading 6
Week 7 What are the Benefits of Generative AI? Analyzes the value and need for Generative AI. Reading 7
Week 8 Popular Generative AI Interfaces. Introduces some popular Generative AI interfaces, such as Dall-E, ChatGPT, and Bard. Reading 8
Week 9 Generative AI Tools. Define the different types of tools of Generative AI, such as text, image, music, code, voice, etc., and their uses. Reading 9
Week 10 What are the Limitations of Generative AI? Examines some of the challenges that Generative AI encounters. Reading 10
Week 11 Negative Implications of Generative AI. Unravel the concerns behind Generative AI. Reading 11
Week 12 Ethics and Bias in Generative AI. Addresses bias and fairness in Generative AI and explores the ethical considerations. Reading 12
Week 13 Generative AI vs. AI. Compares and contrasts Generative AI and AI. Reading 13
Week 14 Making Your Own Generative AI Apps. Teaches how to develop a Generative AI model and/or app. Reading 14
Week 15 Demo your Generative AI Apps! Reading 15
Week 16 Future Prospects of Generative AI. What’s the future of Generative AI? How far can we go? Reading 16
